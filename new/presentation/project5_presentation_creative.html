Preprocessing choices

  • Family mapping: normal, DoS, Probe, R2L, U2R.
  • One-hot encoding for protocol, service, flag.
  • MinMax scaling fitted on training only.
  • Byte logs, traffic ratios, service ratios, error gaps, login anomaly score.

Analysis: R2L and U2R are rare and subtle. A model can look good globally while failing the most important low-frequency attacks, so the report emphasizes per-family recall.

Experimental Design
From baseline to final ensemble

A Structured Pipeline, Not One Isolated Model

1. Baseline

Logistic Regression gives an interpretable reference.

2. Explain

RF + TreeSHAP and Torch MLP + IG expose decision logic.

3. Binary IDS

Attack-vs-normal models optimize operational thresholds.

4. Robustify

Multi-eps PGD fine-tuning and SHAP-guided adversarial augmentation.

5. Ensemble

Adv+ExtraTrees blends neural and tree behavior.

Why the ensemble works: PGD attacks exploit the differentiable Torch surrogate, but those adversarial examples transfer poorly to the tree-heavy final ensemble.

Final weights: 0.15 adversarial Torch + 0.85 SHAP-Robust ExtraTrees4 / 15

Security-OR: Stronger Defense, Lower Clean Balance

How to position it

Security-OR uses calibrated component margins and flags an event when either component is sufficiently confident. It reaches 0.00% transfer-PGD evasion at all tested eps values, but its clean F1 is lower than Adv+ExtraTrees.

Conclusion: mention Security-OR as an optional high-security mode, not as the selected final model.
