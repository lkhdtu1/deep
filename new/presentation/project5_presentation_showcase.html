Preprocessing choices

  • Normal, DoS, Probe, R2L, U2R family mapping.
  • One-hot encoding for protocol, service, and flag.
  • MinMax scaling fitted on training data only.
  • Engineered byte, ratio, error-gap, and login features.

Why this matters: R2L and U2R are rare and subtle. A model can look strong globally while missing these families, so per-family recall is central to the IDS decision.

Technical Toolbox
Explainability, stability, attacks, defenses

Tools Used To Explain, Test, Attack, And Defend

Explainability tools

  • TreeSHAP: exact tree attribution for Random Forest and ExtraTrees-style models.
  • Integrated Gradients: neural attribution from a baseline input to the real input.
  • SmoothIG: repeated noisy IG averaging to reduce unstable local gradients.
  • Combined ensemble explanation: normalized tree attribution plus neural attribution.

Reliability and security tools

  • Local Jaccard: overlap of top features for nearby explanations.
  • Bootstrap Jaccard/rank: consistency across resampled subsets.
  • SHAP/IG-guided evasion: perturb the most important explanation features.
  • PGD and mutable PGD: gradient attacks with full or constrained feature access.
  • Transfer-PGD: craft on Torch surrogate, test on final ensemble.

Why this matters: the methodology follows Project 5 exactly: explain predictions, verify explanation reliability, then analyze whether those explanations create adversarial risk.
